PACHMARHI TRIP

Package Overview

The name Pachmarhi is believed to be derived from the Hindi words Panch ("five") and Marhi ("caves").Pachmarhi, also known as Satpura ki Rani, is a hill station in the central Indian state of Madhya Pradesh. Its many waterfalls include Apsara Vihar, with its calm pool, and soaring, single-drop Silver Fall nearby. The Bee Fall tumbles over a jagged rock face to the northwest. Leopards and Indian bison live in Satpura National Park. The ancient Pandav Caves are a group of 5 dwellings cut out of sandstone on a hill.

Jatashankar Caves

Entry Fees:
Height: 35 meters
IndiaEntry fee: INR 15 per Indian adult.

Jatashankar is a natural cave and Hindu shrine located north of Pachmarhi, in Hoshangabad district, Madhya Pradesh.
Jata means hair and Shankar is another name of Lord Shiva.
There are two ponds fed by springs found in the locality, one of cold water and the other one of hot water.
The cave structure resembles to a hundred head snake named Sheshnag a mythological character.
